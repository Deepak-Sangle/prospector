# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Founders, marketers, and sales/growth leads at small B2B teams who already run their work in Slack. They know their buyers are talking on Reddit, LinkedIn, and X, but they don't have time to babysit search tabs. When they hit this page they're deciding, in under a minute, whether Prospector is worth adding to their workspace. Context: skimming on desktop, often mid-workday, inside or one tab away from Slack.

## Product Purpose

A one-page marketing site for **Prospector** — a social-listening and lead-generation agent that lives entirely in Slack. The page has to make one idea land fast: you describe who to listen for, and qualified leads (with a drafted reply) show up in your channel, without leaving Slack. Success = a visitor understands the create-monitor → scan/filter/draft → lead-in-channel loop and clicks "Add to Slack".

## Brand Personality

Calm, plainspoken, quietly confident. Three words: **grounded, unhurried, sharp**. It talks like a good operator, not a hype deck — no growth-hacking exclamation points, no "10x your pipeline". The prospecting/earth metaphor (digging where the value already is) is carried by warm clay tones and typography, never by literal pickaxe clip-art.

## Anti-references

- The default B2B SaaS look: navy-and-electric-blue, gradient hero, three identical feature cards with rounded-square icons, a big fake dashboard screenshot.
- Growth-hack landing pages shouting fake metrics ("+340% pipeline!") and countdown urgency.
- Generic "AI agent" pages that are all glow, gradients, and glassmorphism with nothing concrete.
- Chatbot-wrapper energy. Prospector solves a real Slack workflow; the page should feel like a considered product, not a demo.

## Design Principles

1. **Show the loop, don't claim it.** Every section makes the monitor → scan/filter/draft → lead-in-channel flow more concrete with real UI mockups, not adjectives.
2. **Honest over impressive.** State real capabilities (3 platforms, 10 keywords, plain-English filters, minimal scopes). No invented performance numbers.
3. **It lives in Slack — so look like it belongs near Slack, not inside a marketing machine.** Warm, editorial, human. Restraint as confidence.
4. **One idea per fold.** Deliberate pacing down a single long scroll; each section earns its space.
5. **Warmth is in the palette and voice, not decoration.** Clay/cream identity (matching the approved reference) carries the mood; typography does the rest.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.1 AA. Body text ≥4.5:1 on the cream background (warm near-black ink, not light gray). Full keyboard operability and visible focus states on the nav, CTAs, and interactive step demos. Every animation has a `prefers-reduced-motion` fallback; content is fully visible without JS or motion. Semantic landmarks and headings throughout.
